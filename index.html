Variations on a Theme

Grady Ward

The building block of a square, with half-circles at the corners, is not new. It is not exciting, it is comprehensible and understood.



I picked it up a week ago after having seen it hidden within a pattern on a wall. However, while solving the graph algorithm buried within the space filling task, (to see if it could be used as an interview question), I was surprised at how simply it could be expressed.

I was also surprised at how even small mistakes in my code or styles led to wildly disparate final products. Once I had fixed my errors, the variations formed by adding 2-3 css classes amazed me.

Edward Elgar's Enigma Variations are one of my favorite pieces of music to listen to and play. Each expresses a theme in the style of someone who was loved by the composer. In their commonality, we find a musical theme of shared humanity. In their contrasts, we see vibrant personality.

I have tried to find new ways of using this old format to portray the personalities of folks that I care for.
