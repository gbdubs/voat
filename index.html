CSS - Variations on a Theme

Grady Ward

A square, with half-circles at the corners, is not new. It is not exciting. It is comprehensible and easy to express.



Thus, I was surprised to find that this simple unit was the driver behind a beautiful and intricate wallpaper in our offices. The space filling algorithm that covered the simplicity of this unit happens to be a classic connected component graph problem. To see if it could be used as an interview question, I coded it up and was surprised at how simply it could be expressed.

I was also surprised at how even small mistakes in my code or styles led to wildly disparate final products. Once I had fixed my errors, I became fascinated with the havoc 2-3 lines of CSS could wreak on the visual result.

Edward Elgar's Enigma Variations are one of my favorite pieces of music to listen to and play. Each expresses a common musical theme in the style of someone who was loved by the composer. In their commonality, we find a shared interconnectivity. In their contrasts, we see vibrant personality.

With this as inspiration, I have experimented with expressing the personalities of folks that I care for with a few lines of CSS. I hope you enjoy the results!
